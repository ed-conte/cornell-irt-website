Behind IRT

Chief Editors

Joel Pang

At IRT Cornell, the sense of community is very clear. This club is a place where diverse minds converge with a common passion for economics and journalism. Members find themselves in a nurturing environment that fosters collaboration and mutual growth. Regular meetings and discussions not only serve as platforms for intellectual exchange but also as grounds for forging lasting friendships. In this community, every voice is valued and every opinion respected, creating an inclusive atmosphere where students from all backgrounds feel at home. Whether it's through engaging in lively conversation, collaborating on writing, or participating in social events, members of IRT Cornell experience a strong sense of belonging and camaraderie. This close-knit community not only enhances the academic journey but also contributes to personal development.

Noah Cordon Siskind

There are many advantages to writing for the IRT Cornell newsletter that go well beyond scholastic advancement. First of all, it gives students a special forum to share their opinions on economic trends and regulations while developing their writing and critical thinking abilities. Since the start of my time in this club, I have realized that the regular content production, required to be a member, hones research skills and instills a habit of keeping up with current affairs in the economy. Furthermore, sending in contributions to the newsletter is a great approach to developing a professional portfolio that will highlight your skills to potential companies or educational institutions. At IRT, contributors, editors, and designers work together to create a coherent and educational newsletter, which fosters collaboration and improves communication and teamwork skills. Writing for the IRT Cornell newsletter is really about being a part of a team that has a significant difference on the larger Cornell community, rather than about solely writing articles.
